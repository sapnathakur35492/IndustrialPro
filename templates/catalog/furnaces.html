01

Electric Resistance Furnace

Electric resistance furnaces represent the cleanest and most controllable solution for aluminium melting and holding applications. Powered by high-grade resistance heating elements, these furnaces deliver precise temperature uniformity across the melt chamber — making them the preferred choice for foundries that demand consistent metallurgical quality with minimal contamination risk. The absence of combustion means no flue gases, no flame impingement, and a significantly cleaner working environment.

Engineered by SKR Engineering for reliability and low maintenance, the electric furnace range is ideally suited for light to medium capacity foundries, die casting units, and gravity casting operations where temperature control and melt quality are non-negotiable. The robust construction ensures long service life with minimal downtime, while the digital control systems allow operators to maintain precise melt temperatures with ease.

02

Gas Fired Furnace

Gas fired aluminium melting furnaces offer a high-throughput, cost-effective solution for foundries operating at medium to large scale where natural gas or LPG supply is readily available. The burner systems are engineered for optimal flame distribution, ensuring rapid melt times while maintaining consistent thermal uniformity throughout the crucible. With lower operating costs compared to electrical alternatives in gas-accessible regions, these furnaces deliver a compelling balance of performance and economy.

SKR Engineering's gas fired furnaces are built for heavy-duty continuous operation — a critical requirement for foundries running multiple shifts. The robust refractory lining, combined with precision burner controls, ensures efficient fuel utilisation and extended furnace life. Safety interlocks and flame failure protection systems are integrated as standard, making these furnaces a reliable backbone for high-volume aluminium melting operations.

03

Oil Fired Furnace

Oil fired furnaces provide a powerful and practical melting solution for foundries in regions where diesel or furnace oil is the most accessible and economical fuel source. These furnaces are engineered to deliver high thermal output with efficient combustion, enabling rapid melt cycles that keep pace with demanding production schedules. The high calorific value of oil-based fuels translates directly into faster heat-up times and sustained melting performance across extended operating periods.

SKR Engineering's oil fired range is designed for durability in demanding foundry environments — built with high-density refractory linings and heavy-duty steel shells to withstand continuous thermal cycling. The burner systems are optimised for complete combustion, reducing fuel waste and operational costs. These furnaces are widely deployed across aluminium casting, die casting, and gravity casting operations where operational flexibility and fuel economy are key priorities.

04

Bale Out Furnace

A bale out furnace is a specialised holding and dispensing furnace designed to maintain molten aluminium at precise casting temperatures and deliver consistent metal to the casting station via ladle or bale-out mechanism. Available in electric, gas, or oil fired configurations, the bale out furnace serves as the critical interface between the melting unit and the casting process — ensuring a steady, temperature-controlled supply of clean molten metal that directly determines casting quality and yield.

The bale out furnace is particularly favoured in high-volume die casting and gravity casting operations where uninterrupted metal supply is essential to production continuity. SKR Engineering's bale out furnaces are engineered with low-turbulence metal chambers that minimise oxide formation and hydrogen pickup — two of the most common causes of casting defects. The result is a cleaner melt, fewer rejections, and a more efficient casting operation overall.

05

Skalner Furnace

The Skalner furnace is an advanced aluminium melting system engineered for superior energy efficiency and minimal metal loss — two parameters that directly impact the profitability of any aluminium foundry. Unlike conventional melting furnaces that expose the charge material directly to the flame or heating elements, the Skalner design utilises a submerged melting principle where the aluminium charge is fed into a heated shaft and melts progressively as it descends — dramatically reducing oxidation losses and dross formation that are inherent in open-bath melting.

This makes the Skalner furnace the preferred choice for operations processing aluminium scrap, returns, and secondary alloys where metal recovery rates are a critical commercial consideration. The combination of reduced dross generation, lower specific energy consumption per kg of metal melted, and high throughput capacity makes the Skalner furnace a significant productivity and profitability upgrade for foundries looking to optimise their melting operations.
