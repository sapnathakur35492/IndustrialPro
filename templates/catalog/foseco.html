About Foseco & Vesuvius

A Legacy Born from Innovation

The story of Foseco begins in 1932 in Birmingham, UK, when Eric Weiss and Dr. Kossy Strauss founded Foundry Services Ltd. Weiss had come to England in 1931 at the age of 22, borrowing just £250 as working capital, armed with foundry flux knowledge acquired while working for his uncle since the age of 16. By 1949, Foseco had grown into an international business, exporting to over 25 countries.

The parallel story of Vesuvius began in 1916, when the Vesuvius Crucible Company was founded in Pittsburgh, USA. In 2008, Foseco was acquired by Cookson plc and merged with Vesuvius. Today Foseco operates as the foundry division of Vesuvius PLC — a global organisation with over a century of combined innovation, serving foundries across more than 30 countries. As an authorised Foseco channel partner, Multi Sales Corporation brings this world-class portfolio directly to foundries across the Maharashtra region.

Fluxes for Aluminium Alloys

Fluxes are a critical consumable in every aluminium foundry — their quality directly determines melt cleanliness, dross generation, oxide control, and ultimately casting quality. Foseco's COVERAL flux range is suitable for covering, cleaning, and drossing applications in aluminium foundries. They reduce oxide formation and build-up on furnace walls, help remove non-metallics from the melt, reduce the aluminium content of dross, and facilitate its removal.

Beyond drossing and covering, Foseco's chemical melt treatment range includes grain refiners (NUCLEANT, TIBORAL, ELDUCTAL series) for microstructure control and SIMODAL modifiers for aluminium-silicon alloy treatment.

03

DYCOTE Die Coatings

Die coatings are essential to the performance and longevity of permanent moulds in gravity and low-pressure die casting operations. The function of the die coating — a semi-permanent layer deposited on the die walls — is to control filling and solidification processes, facilitate release of the casting, and prolong die life. Coating selection and method of application are at least as important as other factors such as die design, alloy type, and metal temperature.

Application Methods: Spray (preferred for dies), brush (runners, risers, fine details), immersion (primarily for copper alloy applications).

04

Degassing Machines (FDU)

Dissolved hydrogen is one of the most damaging contaminants in aluminium melts — it is the primary cause of porosity in castings. Rotary degassing using inert gas is the most effective and efficient method for hydrogen removal, and Foseco's FDU (Foundry Degassing Unit) range sets the global benchmark for this process.

In 1993, Foseco received the Queen's Award for Technological Achievement for the development of the Foundry Degassing Unit. FDU units use the impeller principle with a patented rotor which creates fine inert gas bubbles and mixes them with the melt. These fine bubbles are distributed homogenously throughout the melt without disturbing the surface, maximising the contact area between the gas and the melt.

05

Metal Treatment Station (MTS)

The MTS 1500 is based upon FDU rotary degassing technology, with the additional capability of adding a range of melt treatment products. Fluxes are fed from a dispensing unit into a vortex deliberately created by the spinning rotor — carefully controlled to ensure highly efficient mixing of the treatment products.

Metallurgical: Improved melt cleanliness
Environmental: Reduced consumption
Health & Safety: Reduced operator involvement
Economic: Lower treatment costs

SMARTT Degassing — A software module that predicts the best treatment practice based on ambient conditions, melt temperature, rotor design, and alloy composition.

06

RPT & Density Index Machines

Melt quality cannot be controlled if it cannot be measured. Reduced Pressure Testing (RPT) and Density Index (DI) measurement are the foundry industry's standard methods for quantifying hydrogen content and porosity tendency in aluminium melts before they reach the casting station.

Reduced Pressure Test (RPT)

The RPT machine solidifies a small sample of molten aluminium under reduced atmospheric pressure, causing any dissolved hydrogen to expand and form visible porosity. RPT is fast, simple, and provides immediate feedback.

Density Index (DI)

The Density Index method precisely measures the density of samples solidified at both atmospheric pressure and reduced pressure. A DI below 3–5% is typically acceptable for most casting applications.

Together with Foseco's FDU degassing systems and COVERAL fluxes, RPT and DI machines form a complete melt quality management loop — treat, test, verify, cast.
