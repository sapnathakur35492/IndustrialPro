About Our GDC Machines

Gravity Die Casting (GDC) is one of the most widely used processes for producing high-quality aluminium castings — delivering superior dimensional accuracy, excellent surface finish, and consistent metallurgical properties compared to sand casting. Recognising the growing demand for reliable, affordable, and locally supported GDC equipment across the Maharashtra industrial belt, Multi Sales Corporation has developed its own range of GDC machines in collaboration with Alucast.

Unlike off-the-shelf machines, every GDC machine from Multi Sales Corporation is engineered to the specific requirements of the customer's casting geometry, production volume, and plant layout. From single-cavity prototype tooling to multi-cavity high-volume production setups, our machines are designed, built, and commissioned with direct technical support from our own team.

Horizontal GDC Machine

The horizontal gravity die casting machine is designed for castings where the die parting line runs horizontally — typically suited for flat, disc, or plate-type components where the casting geometry allows for a horizontal split. In horizontal configuration, both die halves are mounted on a horizontal axis, with the moving die traversing laterally to open and close the tool.

This configuration provides excellent access for core setting, insert placement, and casting extraction — making it particularly well-suited for manual or semi-automated operations. Multi Sales Corporation's horizontal GDC machines are built with a rigid fabricated steel frame, precision-ground guide pillars, and hydraulic die actuation for consistent clamping force and smooth die movement.

02

Tilting GDC Machine

The tilting gravity die casting machine is the preferred solution for aluminium castings requiring controlled, low-turbulence metal filling — particularly critical for structural and pressure-tight components where porosity and oxide entrapment must be minimised. In tilting configuration, the entire die assembly rotates from a horizontal filling position to a vertical or near-vertical casting position after metal is poured.

This technique is especially favoured for automotive components, hydraulic housings, brackets, and other safety-critical parts where mechanical properties and internal soundness are paramount. Multi Sales Corporation's tilting GDC machines are engineered with a servo or hydraulic tilt drive, programmable tilt angle and speed control, and a robust counterbalanced frame.

Key Advantages of Tilting GDC
  • Controlled, low-turbulence filling
  • Reduced porosity and oxide entrapment
  • Superior mechanical properties
  • Programmable tilt profiles for process repeatability
