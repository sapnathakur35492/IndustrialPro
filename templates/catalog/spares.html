Industrial Burners

The burner is the heart of any gas fired melting furnace. Its design, fuel efficiency, flame geometry, and combustion control determine how fast metal melts, how uniformly heat is distributed across the crucible, and how much fuel is consumed per heat cycle. SKR Engineering's industrial burners are precision-engineered for their gas fired aluminium melting furnace range.

Designed for operation on natural gas or LPG, these burners feature robust heat-resistant construction and are built to withstand the demanding thermal cycles of continuous foundry operation. Replacement burner assemblies and individual components are available.

Heating Coils

Resistance heating coils are the primary heat-generating element in electric aluminium melting furnaces. They convert electrical energy into radiant and convective heat through high-resistance alloy elements wound or formed to surround the crucible chamber, delivering precise and uniform heating.

SKR Engineering's heating coils are manufactured from high-grade resistance alloys rated for continuous operation at aluminium melting temperatures. Each coil is designed to provide uniform heat distribution around the crucible, minimising hot spots. Available in ratings matched to the full 18 kW – 75 kW furnace range.

Control Panels

Modern aluminium melting furnaces demand precise, consistent control of temperature, power, and operational sequencing. SKR Engineering's control panels are purpose-built for their furnace range, providing centralised management of all critical furnace parameters from a single interface.

The panels are built to industrial standards with robust steel enclosures, clearly labelled instrumentation, and intuitive operator interfaces. Key features include temperature controllers interfaced with thermocouple inputs, power regulation systems (SCR or contactor-based), safety interlocks, and fault indication. Custom panel configurations are available.

04

Temperature Sensors

Accurate temperature measurement is non-negotiable in aluminium melting operations. Even a deviation of 20–30°C from the optimal melt temperature can result in increased dross formation, alloy degradation, or casting defects.

K-Type Thermocouple

The most widely used thermocouple in industrial furnace applications. Directly connected to the furnace control panel, providing a continuous real-time temperature signal that feeds the PID controller.

Dip-Type Thermocouple

Used for quick, direct measurement of molten metal temperature. The sensor tip is briefly immersed into the aluminium melt, providing an accurate spot reading within seconds — essential for verifying actual melt temperature before pouring.

05

Metal Transfer Ladle

The metal transfer ladle is one of the most handled — and most critical — pieces of equipment on the foundry floor. Used to transfer molten aluminium from the melting furnace to the casting station, its design directly affects metal temperature loss during transfer, turbulence and oxide entrapment, operator safety, and the overall cleanliness of the pour.

Multi Sales Corporation supplies robust mild steel or cast iron ladles with refractory lining — designed for regular immersion in molten aluminium at temperatures between 680°C and 780°C. Ladles are available in a range of capacities with fixed handles, bail handles, or crane-lift lugs.
